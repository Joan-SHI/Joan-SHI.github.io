What is your experience of compassionate (or not) leadership?

When I worked in a bank, I was the mentor of new employees in my department. Usually they worked with me for around 1 month before they got used to the Telegraphic Transfer system and worked independently. I think I must have had campassionate leadership then? As we built trust and friendship and learned from each other actually.Till now some of them are still my friends. Here are the 3 components of compassionate leadership mentioned in this book:

  • A cognitive component:"I understand you"
  • An affective component:"I feel for you"
  • A motivational component:"I want to help you"

Do the multiplying goodness meditation. How did you find it?

After I finished the multiplying goodness meditatoin, I felt like I had done it during my childhood. It must be from my mum, a very peaceful woman who set a good model for me. She didn't teach me the following 3 mental habits theory which mentioned in this book but she did give me the practice through the daily life.

  • Seeing goodness in self and others
  • Giving goodness to all
  • Confidence in the transformative power of self(that I can multiply goodness)

Have a difficult conversation. How did it go?

According to this book,there are 5 steps to conduct a difficult conversation:

  • Prepare by walking through the "three conversations"
  • Decide whether to raise the issue
  • Start from the objective "third story"
  • Explore their story and yours
  • Problem solve

    • I would say I am quite good at having a difficult conversation. However I do need to be more careful about "whether to raise the issue or not".
